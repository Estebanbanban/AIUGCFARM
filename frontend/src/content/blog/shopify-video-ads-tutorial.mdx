## Choosing the Right Products

Not every product in your Shopify catalog is equally suited for video advertising. The products you choose to advertise first will significantly impact your initial ROAS and your ability to scale.

### Products That Work Best in Video Ads

**Visually demonstrable products.** If your product does something visible — changes appearance, solves an obvious problem, transforms a space — it will perform well in video because the value proposition is self-evident.

**Products with strong emotional appeal.** Fashion, beauty, pet products, baby products, and gifts all benefit from the emotional connection that video enables. Static images show the product; video shows how the product makes someone feel.

**Products with a $25-$100 price point.** This is the sweet spot for impulse and considered-impulse purchases on social media. Products under $25 can work but need higher volume to hit ROAS targets. Products over $100 typically need longer sales cycles and retargeting sequences.

**Products with clear differentiation.** If your product looks identical to competitors, video gives you the chance to demonstrate what makes it different — better materials, smarter design, superior results.

### Products to Avoid (At First)

- **Commodity products** with no differentiation — video cannot fix a positioning problem
- **Products that require extensive education** — save these for retargeting, not prospecting
- **Low-margin products** — unless your AOV is high enough to absorb ad costs
- **Products with seasonal demand** — time your campaigns to match demand windows

### The Selection Framework

Start by pulling your Shopify analytics and identifying products that meet at least three of these criteria:

1. Top 20% by revenue (proven demand)
2. Gross margin above 60% (room for ad spend)
3. High review count and rating (social proof available)
4. Visually interesting or demonstrable
5. Clear target audience you can describe in one sentence

Pick your top 2-3 products to start. You can always expand later, but starting focused gives you the best chance of early wins.

## Creating Video Ads — 3 Methods

There are three practical methods for creating video ads for your Shopify store. Each has different cost, quality, and speed characteristics.

### Method 1: DIY (Film It Yourself)

**Cost:** $0-$100 (phone + basic lighting)
**Time:** 2-4 hours per video
**Best for:** Store owners who are comfortable on camera, products that are easy to demonstrate

**How to do it:**
1. Use your smartphone — modern phones shoot excellent video
2. Film in natural light or get a $30 ring light from Amazon
3. Use a tripod or phone mount ($15-$25)
4. Film multiple takes of your hook, body, and CTA
5. Edit with free tools like CapCut or InShot
6. Export in 9:16 vertical format

**Tips:** Film in a clean, well-lit space. Speak naturally — pretend you are telling a friend about the product. Do not read from a script; use bullet points as guides. Film more than you think you need.

### Method 2: Hire UGC Creators

**Cost:** $150-$500 per video
**Time:** 1-3 weeks from brief to delivery
**Best for:** Brands with budget who want authentic human content

**How to do it:**
1. Find creators on platforms like Billo, Insense, or Collabstr
2. Write a clear brief specifying your [hook/body/CTA structure](/blog/hook-body-cta-framework)
3. Ship your product to the creator
4. Review the first draft and request revisions
5. Receive final assets with usage rights

**Tips:** Always specify aspect ratio (9:16), duration (15-30 seconds), and key talking points. Send your best-performing product reviews as inspiration. Request raw footage in addition to edited versions so you can create variations.

## Setting Up TikTok Ads (Shopify)

TikTok is one of the most effective platforms for Shopify store owners, especially for products targeting audiences under 40. Here is how to set up your first TikTok ad campaign.

### Step 1: Connect TikTok to Shopify

Install the [TikTok app from the Shopify App Store](https://apps.shopify.com/tiktok). This creates a direct integration between your store and TikTok Ads Manager, including:
- TikTok Pixel installation (automatic)
- Product catalog sync (for dynamic ads)
- Conversion tracking setup

### Step 2: Create Your TikTok Ads Manager Account

If you do not already have one, create a TikTok for Business account at tiktok.com/business. Verify your account with your business information.

### Step 3: Set Up Your First Campaign

1. **Campaign objective:** Choose "Website Conversions" for direct-response e-commerce
2. **Optimization event:** Start with "Add to Cart" if your pixel is new (it needs conversion data to optimize for purchases). Switch to "Complete Payment" once you have 50+ conversion events
3. **Budget:** Set a daily budget of at least $50 per ad group — TikTok's algorithm needs sufficient data to optimize
4. **Targeting:** Start broad (age, gender, location only). Let TikTok's algorithm find your audience through creative targeting
5. **Placements:** Select TikTok only (not the Audience Network) for your first campaigns

### Step 4: Upload Your Creative

Upload your video ads as the creative for your ad group. Best practices for TikTok creative are covered in detail in our [TikTok ad creative strategy guide](/blog/tiktok-ad-creative-strategy-2026).

- Upload 3-5 creative variations per ad group
- Use different hooks across variations
- Include your Shopify product page as the landing page URL
- Write ad copy that complements (not repeats) the video content

### Step 5: Launch and Monitor

Launch your campaign and let it run for at least 3-4 days before making optimization decisions. TikTok's algorithm needs a learning phase to find the right audience. During this phase:
- Do not change budgets, targeting, or creative
- Monitor your hook rate and CTR as leading indicators
- Keep an eye on CPM to ensure you are competitive in auction

## Setting Up Meta Ads (Shopify)

Meta (Facebook and Instagram) offers the largest e-commerce advertising ecosystem and is typically the primary channel for Shopify stores.

### Step 1: Connect Meta to Shopify

Install the Facebook & Instagram app from the Shopify App Store. This connects:
- Meta Pixel (for conversion tracking)
- Conversions API (server-side tracking for better attribution)
- Product catalog (for dynamic ads)

### Step 2: Verify Your Domain

In Meta Business Manager, verify your Shopify store domain. This is required for proper conversion tracking and gives you access to all optimization features.

### Step 3: Configure Your Pixel Events

Ensure these events are tracking correctly:
- ViewContent (product page views)
- AddToCart
- InitiateCheckout
- Purchase

Test each event using the Meta Pixel Helper browser extension or the Events Manager test tool.

### Step 4: Create Your First Campaign

For Shopify stores in 2026, we recommend starting with an Advantage+ Shopping Campaign (ASC):

1. **Campaign type:** Advantage+ Shopping Campaign
2. **Budget:** Start with $50-$100/day
3. **Creative:** Upload 5-10 video ad variations — the more creative options, the better ASC performs
4. **Audience:** ASC handles targeting automatically, but you can set audience suggestions and define existing customer segments
5. **Optimization:** Purchase event (Meta's algorithm has matured enough to optimize for purchases from day one in most cases)

### Step 5: Feed the Algorithm

Meta ASC campaigns perform best with a steady supply of fresh creative. Plan to add 3-5 new creative assets weekly. This is where AI-generated content becomes practically essential — few Shopify stores can sustain that creative volume through traditional production. ## Budgeting & Scaling

The most common question from Shopify store owners starting with video ads: how much should I spend?

### Starting Budget Recommendations

**Minimum viable budget:** $1,000/month across platforms
- $500/month on Meta (primary channel)
- $300/month on TikTok (testing channel)
- $200/month on creative production (AI tools or creator content)

**Recommended starting budget:** $3,000-$5,000/month
- $2,000-$3,000 on Meta
- $1,000-$1,500 on TikTok
- $200-$500 on creative production

**Growth budget:** $10,000+/month
- Scale winning campaigns on Meta and TikTok
- Add YouTube Shorts as a third channel
- Increase creative testing velocity

### The Scaling Framework

Scaling profitably from your starting budget follows a predictable pattern:

**Phase 1: Find winners (Month 1-2).** Test multiple products, hooks, and audience angles with your starting budget. The goal is to identify 2-3 ad creative and product combinations that hit your target CPA.

**Phase 2: Scale winners (Month 2-3).** Increase budget on winning campaigns by 20-30% every 3-4 days. Monitor CPA closely — if it rises more than 20% above target, hold budget and produce fresh creative.

**Phase 3: Expand and diversify (Month 3+).** Add new products, new platforms, and new creative formats. Use winning hook formulas and script structures from Phase 1 as templates for new content. Build a creative production system that delivers fresh assets weekly.

### Creative Budget Allocation

A common mistake is spending 90% of budget on media and 10% on creative. The creative is what determines whether your media spend generates returns.

**Recommended allocation:**
- **85-90% on media spend** (actual ad placement)
- **10-15% on creative production** (AI tools, creator fees, editing)

For a $5,000/month total budget, that means $500-$750 dedicated to creative production. With AI tools like CineRads ($29-$99/month), you can allocate the remaining creative budget to occasional human creator content for diversity.
